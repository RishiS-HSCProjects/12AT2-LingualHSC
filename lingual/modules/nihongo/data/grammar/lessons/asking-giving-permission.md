# ～てもいいです
To ask for permission to do something in Japanese, you can use the ～てもいいですか construction. This is equivalent to asking "Is it okay if I...?" or "May I...?" in English.

:::blockquote Verb (て form) + もいいです [か] :::

To give permission, you can respond with ～てもいいです.

- 窓[まど]を開[あ]けてもいいですか。→ May I open the window?
    はい、開けてもいいです。→ Yes, you may open it.
- けいたい電[でん]話[わ]を使[つか]ってもいいですか。→ Is it okay if I use my mobile phone?
    はい、けいたいを使ってもいいです。→ Yes, you may use your mobile phone.

/w You may sometimes see this structure as "て<strong>は</strong>いいです", which is a more formal way of expressing permission. You will usually use this in written or formal contexts. \

# ～てはいけません
To deny permission or to say that something is not allowed, you can use the ～てはいけません construction. This is equivalent to saying "You must not..." or "It is not allowed to..." in English.

:::blockquote Verb (て form) + はいけません :::

- ここに座[すわ]ってもいいですか。→ May I sit here?
    いいえ、座[すわ]ってはいけません。→ No, you may not sit here.
- 建[たて]物[もの]の中で写[しゃ]真[しん]を撮[と]ってもいいですか。→ Is it okay if I take photos inside the building?
    いいえ、建[たて]物[もの]の中で写[しゃ]真[しん]を撮[と]ってはいけません。でも、外[そと]では撮ってもいいです。→ No, you may not take photos inside the building, but you may take photos outside.


# ~てはだめです
To prohibit something more strongly, you can also use the ～てはだめです construction. This is similar to ～てはいけません but carries a stronger sense of prohibition.

:::blockquote Verb (て form) + はだめです :::
- ビールを飲[の]んでもいいですか。→ Is it okay if I drink beer?
    いいえ、若[わか]者[もの]はビールを飲[の]んではだめです。→ No, young people must not drink beer.
- ここでタバコを吸[す]ってもいいですか。→ May I smoke here?
    いいえ、ここでタバコを吸[す]ってはだめです。→ No, you must not smoke here.
