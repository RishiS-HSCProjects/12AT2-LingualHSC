In Japanese, you can express the difficulty of performing an action using the adjectives やさしい (easy) and にくい (difficult). These adjectives can be attached to the stem of verbs to indicate how easy or difficult it is to perform that action.

:::blockquote Verb (stem) + やさしい / にくい :::

- この本[ほん]は読[よ]み**やすい**です。→ This book is easy to read.
- この料[りょう]理[り]は作[つく]り**やすい**です。→ This dish is easy to make.
- この問題[もんだい]は解決[かいけつ]し**にくい**です。→ This problem is difficult to solve.
- この道[みち]は歩[ある]き**にくい**です。→ This road is difficult to walk on.
