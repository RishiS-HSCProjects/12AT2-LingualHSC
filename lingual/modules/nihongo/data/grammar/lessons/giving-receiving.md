This lesson covers how to express giving and receiving **items** and **favours** in Japanese. In Japanese, giving and receiving expressions depend on viewpoint (whose side the action is described from) and social relationship (equal, senior, junior, or animal/plant).

Before choosing a verb, decide whose action you are describing first:

- Are *you* giving? → [あげる group](#giving-to-someone-else)
- Is *someone else* giving to you? → [くれる group](#someone-giving-to-you)
- Are *you* receiving? → [もらう group](#receiving-from-someone)

:::subject Think in viewpoints first: give from your side (あげる), give toward your side (くれる), receive on your side (もらう). :::

# Giving to someone else
Use this when the giver is you (or your in-group), and the action goes outward to someone else.

- あげます → To give
- さしあげます → To give (formal/respectful)
- やります → To give (to animals, plants, or someone clearly younger/lower)

Base pattern:
:::blockquote [GIVER] は [RECEIVER] に [THING] を あげます／さしあげます／やります :::

Examples:

- しんごくんはまいさんにきれいな花[はな]をあげました → Shingo gave a pretty flower to Mai.
    - まいさん is equal social level here, so あげました is natural.
- しんごくんは花に水[みず]をやりました → Shingo watered the flower.
    - やる is used for plants/animals.
- 私[わたし]は先[せん]生[せい]にレポートをさしあげました → I gave the teacher my report.
    - The receiver is senior, so さしあげました is respectful.

For favours, use the same logic with て-form:
:::blockquote [GIVER] は [RECEIVER] に [Verb (て-form)] あげます／さしあげます／やります :::

# Someone giving to you
Use this when someone does something for you (or for your in-group).

- くれます → To give (to me/us/my side)
- くださいます → To give (respectful)

/w Do not use くれる for actions you do for others. Use あげる-group expressions for that. \

Pattern:
:::blockquote [GIVER] は [RECEIVER (me/us/my side)] に [THING] を くれます／くださいます :::

Examples:

- 友[とも]達[だち]が宿[しゅく]題[だい]を手[て]伝[つだ]いしてくれました → My friend helped me with my homework.
    - Equal relationship, so くれました is natural.
- 先[せん]生[せい]は私[わたし]たちにすしを作[つく]ってくださいました → The teacher made sushi for us (respectful).
    - Teacher is senior, so くださいました is appropriate.
- 母[はは]が新[あたら]しい本[ほん]を買[か]ってくれた → My mother bought me a new book.

For favours, use て-form:
:::blockquote [GIVER] は [RECEIVER (me/us/my side)] に [Verb (て-form)] くれます／くださいます :::

# Receiving from someone
Use this when *you* describe the action as receiving.

/i This is different from くれる. With くれる, the sentence is from the giver's action; with もらう, the sentence is from the receiver's action. \

- もらいます → To receive
- いただきます → To receive (formal/humble)

Pattern:
:::blockquote [RECEIVER] は [GIVER] に [THING] を もらいます／いただきます :::

Examples:

- 私は友[とも]達[だち]にケーキを作[つく]ってもらいました → I received a cake from my friend.
    - Casual/equal relationship, so もらいました.
- 私は先[せん]生[せい]にすしを作[つく]っていただきました → I received sushi from the teacher (respectful).
    - Receiver lowers themselves respectfully, so いただきました.
- 私[わたし]は先[せん]輩[ぱい]にノートを見[み]せてもらいました → I was allowed to see my senior's notes.

For favours, use て-form:
:::blockquote [RECEIVER] は [GIVER] に [Verb (て-form)] もらいます／いただきます :::

/t いただきます is also said before eating to show gratitude and humility toward the people who prepared the meal. \

# Favours

:::blockquote Giver + は・が + receiver + に + Verb (て-form) + [To Give・To Receive] :::

## Doing a favour for someone else

/i ～てさしあげます is used when the receiver is senior to the person giving \
/i ～てやります is used when the receiver is an animal, plant, or someone younger. \

/w Using てあげます can sound boastful and therefore should be avoided. \

In Japanese, it is often more natural not to emphasise that you "did a favour" for someone. If the favour meaning is obvious from context, plain past form can sound softer.

For example, use 料[りょう]理[り]しました rather than 料[りょう]理[り]してあげました when you do not need to stress "for someone".

- しんごくんはまいちゃんにきれいな花[はな]を買[か]ってあげました → Shigo bought a pretty flower for Mai.
- しんごくんは花に水[みず]をやりました → Shigo watered the flower.
- 私[わたし]は弟[おとうと]に漢[かん]字[じ]を教[おし]えてやりました → I taught kanji to my younger brother.

## Receiving a favour from someone.

### てくださいます
てくださいます is the respectful form of くれる. Use it when the giver is senior to you or when you want to show respect.


/i ～てくれました is for general usage. \
/i ～てくださいます is for when the person is more senior to you. \

- 先[せん]生[せい]は私[わたし]たちにすしを作[つく]ってくださいました → The teacher made sushi for us (respectful).
- けんくんのお父[とう]さんはぼくにしゃしんを送[おく]ってくださいました。→ Ken's father sent me a photo (respectful).
- 部[ぶ]長[ちょう]が私[わたし]にアドバイスしてくださった → The team leader kindly gave me advice.


To say that someone does a favor for you or for someone close to you in Japanese:

:::blockquote Verb (て-form) + くれる (any tense) :::

/w You can not use くれる to talk about favors you have done for others. To express that, you will need to use ～てあげる (which means 'to give') instead. \

For example:

- 友[とも]達[だち]が宿[しゅく]題[だい]を手伝[てつだ]いしてくれました → My friend helped me with my homework.
- 母[はは]が新[あたら]しい本[ほん]を買[か]ってくれた → My mother bought me a new book.
- 聞いてくれてありがとう。 → Thank you for (doing me the favour of) listening.
- 手[て]伝[つだ]ってくれて助[たす]かりました。 → Thanks for helping me; that really helped.

/t This last phrase is really handy to remember if you ever need to give a speech for an assessment! \
