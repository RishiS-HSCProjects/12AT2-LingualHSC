なさい is usually used by parents to children or teachers to students to give commands or instructions. It is a polite but firm way to tell someone to do something.

For example, a parent might say to their child:

- 宿[しゅく]題[だい]をしなさい。→ Do your homework.
- 早[はや]く寝[ね]なさい。→ Go to bed early.
- 部[へ]屋[や]をそうじしなさい。→ Clean your room.

:::blockquote Verb (stem) + なさい :::

For example:

| Dictionary Form | ます-Form | Verb Stem | なさい Form | Meaning |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| 食[た]べる | 食べます | 食べ | 食べなさい | Eat |
| 行[い]く | 行きます | 行き | 行きなさい | Go |
| 書[か]く | 書きます | 書き | 書きなさい | Write |
| 読[よ]む | 読みます | 読み | 読みなさい | Read |
| 見[み]る | 見ます | 見 | 見なさい | Watch/See |

/w Many students make the mistake of treating なさい as if it is an extension of the な conjunction, resulting in incorrect forms like 行かなさい and 書かなさい. \
