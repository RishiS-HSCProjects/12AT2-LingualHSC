---

title: "Expressing Purpose with ～ために"
summary: "Learn how to express purpose in Japanese using the ～ために structure."

---

To say "for the purpose of" or "in order to" in Japanese, you can use the structure ～ために. This grammar point is used to indicate the purpose of an action.

:::blockquote Verb (plain present) + ために :::
/t Use the plain present form of the verb before ために to indicate the purpose of doing that action. \

- やさいを買[か]う**ために**スーパーに行きました → I went to the supermarket to buy vegetables.
- 日本語を勉[べん]強[きょう]する**ために**図[と]書[しょう]館[かん]に行きます → I go to the library to study Japanese.
- 友[とも]達[だち]に会[あ]う**ために**電[でん]話[わ]します → I will call to meet my friend.

:::blockquote Noun + の + ために :::
- けんこうの**ために**自[じ]転[てん]車[しゃ]で学校に通[かよ]います → I commute to school by bicycle for my health.
- 家族[かぞく]の**ために**働[はたら]きます → I work for my family.
- 地[ち]球[きゅう]の**ために**リサイクルをします → I recycle for the sake of the Earth.
