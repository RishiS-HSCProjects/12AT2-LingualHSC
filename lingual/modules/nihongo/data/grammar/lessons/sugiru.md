---

title: "Using すぎる to Indicate Excessiveness"
summary: "Learn how to use the auxiliary verb すぎる (sugiru) in Japanese to express the idea of 'too much' or 'excessiveness' with both verbs and adjectives."

---

To indicate that an action or quality is excessive in Japanese, you can use the *auxiliary verb* すぎる (sugiru). This grammar point can be applied to both verbs and adjectives.

:::blockquote Verb (stem) + すぎる :::
/t The verb stem is the verb without its ます ending. \

- 食べすぎた → I ate too much.
- おさけを飲[の]みすぎないでください。→ Please don't drink too much alcohol.
- 勉強[べんきょう]しすぎて、疲[つか]れました。→ I studied too much and got tired.
/t Remember すぎる is a Ichidan verb, so you can easily conjugate it accordingly. \

:::blockquote い-adjective (remove い) + すぎる :::

- 高[たか]すぎる → too expensive
- 早[はや]すぎる → too fast
- さむすぎて、外[そと]に出[で]られない。→ It's too cold to go outside.

:::blockquote な-adjective + すぎる :::

- 静[しず]かすぎる → too quiet
- 便利[べんり]すぎる → too convenient
- 彼[かれ]は親切[しんせつ]すぎて、困[こま]ることもある。→ He is too kind, which can sometimes be troublesome.
