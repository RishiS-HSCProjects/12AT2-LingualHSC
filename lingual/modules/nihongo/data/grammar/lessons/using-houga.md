In Japanese, you can express the idea of "had better" or "it is better to" do something by using the ～ほうがいい structure. This is useful for giving advice or recommendations.

:::blockquote Verb (た-Form) + 方[ほう]がいい :::

- 早[はや]くねた方[ほう]がいいです → It is better to go to bed early.
- 水[みず]を飲[の]んだ方[ほう]がいいです → It is better to drink water.
- 運[うん]動[どう]した方[ほう]がいいです → It is better to exercise.

:::blockquote Verb (ない-Form) + 方[ほう]がいい :::

- あまり食[た]べない方[ほう]がいいです → It is better not to eat too much.
- タバコを吸[す]わない方[ほう]がいいです → It is better not to smoke.
- 夜[よる]遅[おそ]くまで起[お]きない方[ほう]がいいです → It is better not to stay up late.
