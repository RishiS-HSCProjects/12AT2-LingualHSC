ないで is a Japanese grammatical structure used to indicate that one action is not performed before or while another action takes place.　This form has various uses, including expressing actions done without doing something else, making requests, and indicating simultaneous actions.

:::blockquote Verb (ない-form) + で :::

# Using 〜ないで to Express 'Without Doing Something'

In Japanese, the 〜ないで form is used to express the idea of 'without doing something.' It indicates that one action is not performed before or while another action takes place. This form is commonly used in both casual and formal contexts.

- そうじを**しないで**、テレビを見ました。→ Instead of cleaning, I watched TV.
- 朝ごはんを**食[た]べないで**、学校に行きました。→ I went to school without eating breakfast.
- 傘[かさ]を**持[も]たないで**、出かけました。→ I went out without bringing an umbrella.

Additionally, 〜ないで can be combined with other verbs to express simultaneous actions or conditions. For example:
  
- 音[おん]楽[がく]を聞[き]かないで、勉[べん]強[きょう]します。→ I study without listening to music.
- 電[でん]話[わ]をかけないで、メールを送[おく]ります。→ I will send an email without making a phone call.


# Using 〜ないでください to Make Requests
The 〜ないで form can also be used to make requests or give instructions in a polite manner. For example:

- 静[しず]かに**しないで**ください。→ Please do not be noisy.
- 遅[おそ]く**来[こ]ないで**ください。→ Please do not come late.
