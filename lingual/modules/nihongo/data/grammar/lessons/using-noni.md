---

title: "Using のに"
summary: "Learn how to use the Japanese conjunction のに to express contrast or unexpected outcomes in sentences."

---

のに is used to express contradiction, when something goes against expectation, to highlight a situation that the speaker is **dissatisfied with or disappointed** by.

:::blockquote Verb (plain form, any tense) + のに :::

- 学校へ行けたのに、行かなかった → Even though I was able to go to school, I didn't go.
- 雨[あめ]が降[ふ]っているのに、彼は傘を持っていない → Even though it's raining, he doesn't have an umbrella.

:::blockquote い-adjective + のに :::

- 安[やす]いのに、買[か]わなかった → Even though it was cheap, I didn't buy it.
- 暑[あつ]いのに、水[みず]を飲[の]みませんでした → Even though it was hot, I didn't drink water.

:::blockquote な-adjective + な のに :::

- ギーターを引[ひ]くのは上手[じょうず]なのに、ぜんぜんパフォーマンスしない → Even though he is good at playing the guitar, he doesn't perform at all.
- 静[しず]かなのに、彼女はよく怒[おこ]る → Even though she is quiet, she often gets angry.

:::blockquote Noun + な のに :::

- 彼は医者[いしゃ]なのに、ニュロフィンを使い方がわからない → Even though he is a doctor, he doesn't know how to use Neurophin.
- 学生[がくせい]なのに、毎[まい]日[にち]バイトをしている → Even though he is a student, he does a part-time job every day.
